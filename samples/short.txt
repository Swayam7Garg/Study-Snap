Photosynthesis is a fundamentally important biological process used by plants, algae, and certain bacteria to harness energy from sunlight and turn it into chemical energy. The essential process involves capturing light energy and using it to convert water, carbon dioxide, and minerals into oxygen and energy-rich organic compounds, primarily glucose. This chemical reaction takes place in the chloroplasts, tiny structures within the cells of leaves that contain the green pigment chlorophyll. Chlorophyll is responsible for absorbing the solar radiation needed to drive the reaction. Photosynthesis is responsible for maintaining the oxygen levels in Earth's atmosphere and supplies all of the organic compounds and most of the energy necessary for life on Earth. A typical photosynthetic equation can be summarized as six molecules of carbon dioxide and six molecules of water, plus light energy, yielding one molecule of glucose and six molecules of oxygen gas.
